  - Routine child-health visits and ward reviews

  - Follow-up of at-risk children over time
equipment:
  - Growth charts and age-appropriate anthropometry tools
  - Reliable age information and caregiver history
  - A framework for milestone review
keySteps:
  - Measure and plot growth parameters accurately rather than relying on memory
  - Ask milestone questions appropriate to the child's age and context
  - Look for patterns over time instead of interpreting one isolated number
  - Escalate when delay, regression, or major discordance is suspected
pitfalls:
  - Using rough estimates instead of accurate measurements
  - Missing developmental regression because only current ability was asked about
  - Failing to interpret growth in relation to age, sex, and serial trend

## Practical frame

Students should treat growth charts and milestone review as clinical tools, not as community-medicine paperwork.

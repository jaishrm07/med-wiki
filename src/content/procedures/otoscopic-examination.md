  - Ear pain, fever, hearing symptoms, or discharge

equipment:
  - Working otoscope with appropriate speculum
  - Adequate light and patient positioning
  - A clear explanation to reduce patient movement and discomfort
keySteps:
  - Inspect the pinna and external canal before advancing the otoscope
  - Hold the otoscope steadily and align the canal gently
  - Describe canal and tympanic membrane findings before naming the likely diagnosis
  - Compare with the opposite ear when clinically relevant
pitfalls:
  - Pushing too quickly and causing pain
  - Calling every red membrane infected without considering technique and context
  - Forgetting to describe what was actually seen

## Practical frame

Students should aim for a stable, comfortable examination that produces a usable description rather than a rushed glimpse.

equipment:
  - Adequate light and patient positioning
  - A structured sequence for inspection, palpation, and neck observation
  - Thyroid-test context if available, without letting it replace the exam
keySteps:
  - Inspect the neck and observe swallowing before palpation
  - Palpate the gland systematically and describe size, symmetry, and nodularity
  - Look for associated systemic clues that support thyroid dysfunction
  - Relate findings back to whether the case needs testing, follow-up, or referral
pitfalls:
  - Treating normal neck fullness as clear thyroid enlargement without careful palpation
  - Forgetting to describe what was found
  - Letting test results replace the need for a proper examination

## Practical frame

The right goal is a clear, reproducible description of the gland and its clinical context, not a dramatic diagnosis from one glance.

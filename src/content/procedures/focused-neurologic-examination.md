  - Reflex hammer and basic bedside examination tools
  - Adequate space and privacy for gait and coordination review

keySteps:
  - Start with higher functions, speech, and overall observation
  - Assess cranial nerves, motor system, reflexes, coordination, and sensation in a planned sequence
  - Localize abnormalities rather than listing unrelated findings
  - Recheck any abnormal finding that will change urgency or localization
pitfalls:
  - Jumping to diagnosis without documenting the actual deficit
  - Performing an incomplete exam and calling it normal
  - Forgetting that new focal deficits change the urgency of care

## Practical frame

The student-level goal is not a full neurology textbook exam. It is a reliable focused sequence that detects findings worth acting on.

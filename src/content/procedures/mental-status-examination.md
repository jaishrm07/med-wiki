maturity: reviewed
lastReviewed: 2026-04-05
indications:
  - Low mood, altered behavior, suspiciousness, or other psychiatric presentations
  - Ward, OPD, and bedside psychiatric assessment under supervision
  - Follow-up of known psychiatric illness for change in symptoms and functioning
equipment:
  - Quiet setting with privacy
  - Enough time for observation and focused questioning
  - A framework for documenting findings clearly
keySteps:
  - Observe appearance, behavior, and rapport from the start of the encounter
  - Assess speech, mood, affect, and thought systematically
  - Explore perception, cognition, insight, and judgment in context
  - Record what was observed and what the patient reported as separate but related data
pitfalls:
  - Turning the examination into a checklist without listening to the patient
  - Using diagnostic labels in place of described findings
  - Ignoring risk, functioning, or impaired insight

## Practical frame

Students should be able to explain how the findings support or weaken a working diagnosis, not just recite the domains of examination.

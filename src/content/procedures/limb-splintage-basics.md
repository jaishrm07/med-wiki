  - Appropriate splinting material and padding
  - Bandage or securing material

keySteps:
  - Assess the limb and distal neurovascular status before splintage
  - Immobilize the injured area in a position that protects function and pain control
  - Secure the splint without creating pressure or circulatory compromise
  - Reassess pain, perfusion, and sensation after splintage
pitfalls:
  - Forgetting to document distal pulses and sensation
  - Applying a tight splint that worsens swelling-related risk
  - Treating splintage as definitive treatment rather than temporary protection

## Practical frame

The student-level goal is safe immobilization, repeated neurovascular checking, and clear escalation for definitive fracture care.

  - Need for intravenous fluids, drugs, or emergency access

equipment:
  - Appropriate IV cannula and extension set if used locally
  - Tourniquet, antiseptic, gloves, dressing, and flush materials
  - Sharps disposal and a plan for securing the line
keySteps:
  - Prepare equipment first so the attempt is controlled rather than improvised
  - Identify a suitable vein, apply aseptic preparation, and insert with attention to angle and stabilization
  - Confirm intravascular placement before securing the cannula
  - Label and monitor the site after insertion
pitfalls:
  - Starting before all equipment is ready
  - Breaking aseptic technique during a difficult attempt
  - Leaving a newly placed cannula unsecured or undocumented

## Practical frame

Students should think through indication, asepsis, line security, and aftercare as one workflow rather than treating insertion as the whole task.

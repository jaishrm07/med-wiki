coreUses:
  - Early management of dehydration from acute diarrheal illness
  - Oral fluid replacement when the patient can still drink safely
  - Community and ward-level supportive care for common gastrointestinal losses
majorCautions:
  - Severe dehydration or shock needs urgent escalation and often IV fluids
  - Ongoing vomiting can limit oral therapy
  - ORS helps when used correctly, not when taken sporadically or too little
commonAdverseEffects:
  - Occasional vomiting if taken too quickly
  - Poor tolerance when severe illness makes oral intake difficult
  - Little clinical benefit if mixed or used incorrectly
keyPoints:
  - ORS works because sodium-glucose transport remains usable even during diarrhea
  - It should be offered early rather than after dehydration becomes severe
  - Teaching caregivers how to use it is part of the treatment

## Practical frame

ORS is most useful educationally when students understand why it works and when it stops being enough.

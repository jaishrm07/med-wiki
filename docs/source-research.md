# Source Research

This document defines the preferred source hierarchy for Med Wiki.

## Source hierarchy

### Tier 1: Primary and official

Use these first whenever available:

- NMC curriculum and competency documents
- national standard treatment guidelines
- specialty society guidelines
- WHO, CDC, NIH, NICE, ICMR, MoHFW, and other official health bodies
- pharmacopoeias, essential medicines lists, vaccine schedules, and public-health manuals

These are the highest-priority sources for clinical claims, practice guidance, and curriculum alignment.

### Tier 2: Standard textbooks

Use these to support student learning structure:

- anatomy texts
- physiology texts
- biochemistry texts
- pathology texts
- pharmacology texts
- medicine and surgery references

These are especially useful for explanation quality, sequence, and exam-facing framing.

### Tier 3: Secondary explainers

Use carefully and only as support:

- reputable medical education websites
- review articles
- teaching notes
- exam-oriented summaries

Do not let these outrank official guidance or standard references.

## Data collection workflow

For each new source:

1. save the source in `raw/` or save a durable note that points to it
2. create a vault note with:
   - title
   - source type
   - publication or update date
   - why it matters
   - what pages it should influence
3. update related topic, subject, or system notes
4. decide whether the public website needs to change

## Medical-specific guardrails

- Keep date stamps on anything that could go stale.
- Distinguish student-study explanation from point-of-care recommendation.
- Keep a clear note when advice is guideline-based versus textbook-based.
- Flag disagreements between sources instead of flattening them away.
